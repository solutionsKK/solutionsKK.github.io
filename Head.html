GPS Head

If smart tech isn’t going anywhere, its ethical tensions aren’t either. Aisyah Shah Idil asks if our pleasantly tactile gadgets are taking more than they give.

When we call a device ‘smart’, we mean that it can:

  • Learn and adapt to human behaviour
  • Make decisions independently
  • Communicate wirelessly with other devices

Examples include smart locks, robot vacuums like the Roomba, and even wearable payment rings and sleep-enhancing headbands.

Did you know? The SmartSleep headband helps improve deep sleep using white noise!

Brain-Hacking

Thync, a wearable tech startup, goes beyond tracking by offering instant symptom relief via gentle electric pulses.

Their tech claims to:

  1. Stimulate neurons using low-voltage electric pulses
  2. Control the release of mood-related brain chemicals
  3. Boost mental focus and emotional well-being
